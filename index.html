The dark side of free speech

Free speech is one of the most important humans rights and one of the most misunderstood. The preservation of free speech right is the result of the difficult equilibrium with what is done to contain hate. “Hate speech” cannot be easily defined, but by agreement it expresses hate and intolerance towards a person or a group. The consequenses can range from mere bullying or intimidation to actual violent events.

EU Steps for fighting online hate speech

Many EU countries believe that hate speech might be prevented by restricting free speech. One of the hardest battleground is online, because of its characteristics of permanence, transnationality, unpredictability and anonymity.

The Code of Conduct

The IT Companies do not follow territorial legislations but role models - called policy - that make them proceed through the filtering process or the removal of improper contents from each platform. The most relevant in EU is the Code of Conduct.

What are they filtering for?

Twitter

Freedom of expression means little if voices are silenced because people are afraid to speak up. We do not tolerate behavior that harasses, intimidates, or uses fear to silence another person’s voice.

Facebook

Non permettiamo su Facebook discorsi di incitazione all'odio perché creano un ambiente di intimidazione ed esclusione e, in alcuni casi, possono promuovere violenza reale.

YouTube

We encourage free speech and try to defend your right to express unpopular points of view, but we don't permit hate speech. There is a fine line between what is and what is not considered to be hate speech.

The journey of a reported content

Two are the main important elements of effective filtering: the time of assessment of the notifications by the moderator, and the effective decision that establishes that a content is hateful or not.

How does it end?

During the three monitorings, as the problem became more and more visible, there had been a relatively increasing number of notifications that at the end of the process have been actually removed.

Hateful categories

Since hate is generated in different contexts, it doesn’t have a unique target. Social Networks have only been a means of proliferation for different kind of hatred, but the different sympathies and policies of the companies have given more importance to some groups despite others.

Hate words

Hatebase is a tool that aims to map the way hate spreads: the words it contains help people recognize hate episodes both online and in real life, better understand that they can cause moral damage, and that their censorship is in many contexts understandable.
